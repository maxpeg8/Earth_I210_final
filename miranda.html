Miranda

Miranda is the fifth largest moon orbiting Uranus but is the smallest of Uranus's five major moons. It is known for its rugged terrain, including canyons and areas that appear to have been heavily impacted by geological activity. Some of its canyons are 12 times deeper than the Grand Canyon. It takes this moon just over 1 day on Earth to orbit the planet. Miranda was discovered in 1948.

Mass:

6.4x10^19 kg

Diameter:

471 km

Materials:

Ice and rock with many canyons

Distance:

129,000 km to Uranus

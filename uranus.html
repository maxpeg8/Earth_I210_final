Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet in the solar system and is one of the gas giants. It is pale blue due to methane in its atmosphere. Uranus is a unique planet because it rotates on its side, with a tilt of about 98 degrees. This tilt causes each pole to experience 42 years of sunlight, then 42 years of darkness. The planet's atmosphere is composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane. It also has faint rings as well as 28 known moons.
